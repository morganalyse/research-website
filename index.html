Evolution of Mobile Cellphones

Research By: Morgan McDonald

Motorola DynaTAC: 1983

The date was April 3, 1973, forty years ago a Motorola exec made what is considered an idea to be the initial call on what we would recognize now as the first truly mobile phone. DynaTAC was a merged acronym for Dynamic Adaptive Total Area Coverage. He used it to call a rival. It was the first commercially-available mobile phone, costing about $4,000 U.S. This mobile phone could also hold up to 30 minutes of available talking time. There are many different things to describe this cellphone. It weighed about 784g and it was not touchscreen, the dimensions were 3300 x 898 x 445mm, and its volume was .1318710 cm3. When wanting to recharge it costed $3,995 and it took as long as 10 hours. It was also the first to carry a phone call over the regular phone system. But the first true cellular call was also made on a Motorola DynaTAC — to an Ameritech Mobile exec named Bob Barnett in 1983.

Nokia N1011: 1992

Just 15 years go the Nokia 1011 was made. It was the first mass produced GMS cellphone. The Nokia 1011 was based on the similar looking analogue Nokia 101. The name 1011 referred to the day and month of release: 10 November. The dimensions 195 x 60 x 45mm (with standard battery and internal antenna). The 475g 1011 was rather heavier than today’s models. It could be also listed as a two-way call screen. The phone was about to send and receive messages. It can hold 99 contacts and you could have a total of 90 minutes. The charge holds for about 12 hours if it is in stand-by mode. There was no camera, Bluetooth, memory card, no color. It did introduce messaging. There also was a ringtone but the jingle wasn’t available until 1994. The first GSM was in mass production and it was operated in the 900MHz.

Nokia 6110: 1997

Nokia 6110 was launched nearly 20 years ago and to be specific it was the 12th of February. The dimensions are 130 x 47 x 28 mm, 130 cc (5.12 x 1.85 x 1.10 in) and it’s weight is 137g. There is no camera but it’s has a alarm, messages, call, three games, and more. There is a removable battery that will allow you to talk for about 2 hours to 3 hours and 20 minutes but most importantly it hold a charge for about 40 hours to 120 hours. there is also a thing called a standby battery which had 60 hours to 270 hours of battery. It comes in 2 different colors and it can hold up to 50 contacts. When calling there was no aloud speaker so there couldn’t be anything more than just putting it up to your ear.

Nokia 3310: 2000

The Nokia 3310 is a mobile cellphone announced on 1 September 2000. It is a slightly rounded rectangular unit that is typically held in the palm of a hand, with the buttons operated with the thumb. The Nokia 3310's main new feature introduced over its predecessors was a chat function. The 3310 is known for having many features that were rare for the time. The blue button is the main button for selecting options, with "C" button as a "backspace", "back" or "undo" button. These include many utilities, such as a calculator, stopwatch, and nokia network monitor and a reminder function. Its dimensions are 113 mm × 48 mm × 22 mm (4.45 in × 1.89 in × 0.87 in). It costed $26-$177 and you have a contact with your provider for 2 years.

Apple Iphone: 2007

Originally as you see today we have created the Iphone10, soon be an iPhone 11. So let’s go back to 2007. The first ever “IPhone”, known as the Iphone1. Famous inventor Steven P. Jobs introduced this apple product to the world on January 9th of 2007. Featuring a new input technology called “Multi-Touch” the iPhone features only one physical button, called “home.” You control the phone by sliding a finger across its touch-sensitive 3.5-inch display, which has a resolution of 320-by-480 pixels at 160 pixels-per-inch display. This phone also introduced many apps and data to the world. For example, photo, contacts, notes, safari, music and videos, etc. Despite the phone’s features, he said that the phone’s strongest feature was its improvement of how calls are made. An easy use of call feature lets users connect calls with touch of the screen. The iPhone came in two different versions: a 4GB, which was $499 and an 8GB, which was $599. Both required a two-year contract with your exclusive U.S. carrier.
